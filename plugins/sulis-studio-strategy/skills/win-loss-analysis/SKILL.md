# Win/Loss Analysis Skill

> **Purpose:** Systematically analyze won and lost deals to extract actionable
> insights for product, sales, marketing, and competitive strategy.
>
> **Lifecycle Phase:** Operate (post-launch feedback loop)
> **Reference:** Competitive intelligence and voice-of-customer methodology

### Root Cause Categories

| Category | Description | Example Reasons |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Product** | Capability or feature related | Missing feature, integration gap, scale limits |
| **Price** | Cost or commercial terms | Too expensive, inflexible terms, no ROI |
| **Process** | Sales or buying process | Slow response, poor demo, missed stakeholder |
| **Competition** | Competitor advantage | Better feature, stronger relationship, lower price |
| **Fit** | Misalignment with needs | Wrong segment, premature, overengineered |
| **Timing** | External timing factors | Budget freeze, priority shift, too early/late |
| **Relationship** | Trust and confidence | Weak references, brand concerns, support doubts |

## Win/Loss Interview Framework

### Interview Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                         WIN/LOSS INTERVIEW GUIDE                             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                              │
│  PHASE 1: CONTEXT (5 min)                                                   │
│  → Confirm role and involvement in decision                                 │
│  → Understand their evaluation timeline                                     │
│  → Identify other stakeholders involved                                     │
│                                                                              │
│  PHASE 2: PROCESS (10 min)                                                  │
│  → What triggered the evaluation?                                           │
│  → What alternatives did you consider?                                      │
│  → What were your top 3 evaluation criteria?                                │
│  → Walk me through your decision process                                    │
│                                                                              │
│  PHASE 3: PERCEPTION (10 min)                                               │
│  → How did you perceive our product vs alternatives?                        │
│  → What stood out positively? Negatively?                                   │
│  → How did you perceive our pricing?                                        │
│  → What was your confidence level in our ability to deliver?                │
│                                                                              │
│  PHASE 4: DECISION (5 min)                                                  │
│  → What was the primary reason for your decision?                           │
│  → What would have changed your decision?                                   │
│  → If you could give us one piece of advice, what would it be?              │
│                                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Question Bank by Category

#### Product Questions
- "How well did our product meet your functional requirements?"
- "Were there any must-have features we were missing?"
- "How did you rate our product's ease of use?"
- "How did our integrations compare to alternatives?"
- "Did our roadmap align with your future needs?"

#### Competitive Questions
- "Which alternatives did you seriously consider?"
- "How did we compare to {competitor} on {criterion}?"
- "Was there an incumbent? What would it take to displace them?"
- "Did you have a pre-existing preference? What shaped it?"

#### Commercial Questions
- "How did our pricing compare to your expectations?"
- "How did you calculate ROI for this decision?"
- "Were our contract terms acceptable?"
- "What pricing model would have been ideal?"

#### Process Questions
- "How would you rate your experience with our sales team?"
- "Did you get the information you needed during evaluation?"
- "Were there any delays or friction in our process?"
- "Did you feel understood by our team?"

#### Trust Questions
- "What gave you confidence (or concern) about working with us?"
- "Did you speak with references? What did you learn?"
- "How did you perceive our company's stability/trajectory?"
- "What would have increased your confidence in us?"

## Analysis Workflow

### Step 1: Data Collection

```
Sources for Win/Loss Data:
├── CRM (deal records, notes)
├── Sales team debriefs
├── Customer interviews (wins)
├── Lost prospect interviews
├── Churn exit interviews
└── Support/success feedback
```

### Step 2: Deal Selection

Prioritize interviews for:
- Large deals (high impact)
- Strategic segments (beachhead)
- Competitive losses (intelligence)
- Recent decisions (fresh memory)
- Churned customers (retention insights)

### Step 3: Interview Execution

| Interview Type | Timing | Interviewer | Duration |
|----------------|--------|-------------|----------|
| Win Interview | 30-60 days post-close | Product/Success | 20-30 min |
| Loss Interview | 14-30 days post-decision | Product/External | 20-30 min |
| Churn Interview | Within 30 days | Success/External | 20-30 min |

### Step 4: Analysis

1. Code each deal by root cause category
2. Identify patterns across deals
3. Quantify themes (# mentions, # deals)
4. Cross-reference with segment/competitor
5. Triangulate with other data sources

### Step 5: Action Planning

| Finding Type | Action Owner | Action Type |
|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| Product gap | Product | Feature/Roadmap |
| Competitive weakness | Product + Marketing | Battlecard + Feature |
| Sales process issue | Sales | Training + Process |
| Pricing concern | Leadership | Pricing review |
| Messaging gap | Marketing | Content + Positioning |

## Integration with OFM

### Feedback Loop to Features

```
Win/Loss Finding
    │
    ├── Product Gap → Feature Request
    │   └── features/{gap}/  (new feature)
    │
    ├── Competitive Intel → Competitive Research
    │   └── product/research/competitive/{competitor}/
    │
    ├── Pricing Insight → COMMERCIAL.md
    │   └── Pricing validation
    │
    └── Market Signal → Journey Validation
        └── product/offerings/primary/journeys/{journey}/ MARKET_EVIDENCE.md
```

### Triggering Feature Work

When win/loss analysis identifies a product gap:

1. Document in `product/research/win-loss/themes/product-gaps.md`
2. Create feature request with evidence
3. Link to win/loss analysis as MARKET_EVIDENCE
4. Proceed through feature lifecycle
