# Company Research Skill

> **Purpose:** Conduct systematic, fact-checked company research for business development,
> producing consistent, scored outputs with Harvard-referenceable sources.

---

## Critical Design Principles

**The Company Research Skill produces reliable, actionable intelligence for BD.**

This ensures:
- **Accuracy** - Every fact is verified and scored for confidence
- **Consistency** - Output follows a standardised template every time
- **Traceability** - Every claim has a cited source with verification status
- **Actionability** - Research directly supports BD decision-making

## Confidence Scoring Framework

> **Every fact must be scored for confidence based on source quality and verification.**

### Confidence Levels

| Score | Level | Criteria | Use Case |
|-------|-------|----------|----------|
| **5** | Verified | Multiple Tier 1-2 sources agree; official confirmation | Can state as fact |
| **4** | High | Single Tier 1-2 source OR multiple Tier 3 sources agree | Can state with high confidence |
| **3** | Moderate | Single Tier 2-3 source; no contradictions found | State with caveat |
| **2** | Low | Single Tier 3-4 source; limited verification | Flag as unverified |
| **1** | Unverified | Single unverified source; speculation | Note as rumour/speculation |
| **0** | Not Found | No reliable source found | Mark as "Unknown" |

### Verification Requirements

| Fact Type | Minimum Score | Verification Method |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Founding date | 4 | Companies House / Crunchbase / LinkedIn |
| Founder names | 4 | LinkedIn + Company website |
| Technical co-founder | 4 | LinkedIn background + education verification |
| Funding amounts | 4 | Press release + Crunchbase/PitchBook |
| B2B/B2C classification | 4 | Website analysis + customer evidence |
| Customer names | 3 | Case studies, testimonials, press |
| Valuation | 3 | Press coverage (often estimated) |
| Revenue | 2 | Usually not public; flag as estimated |

## Source Credibility Tiers

### Tier 1: Official/Authoritative Sources (Highest Credibility)

**What:** Government registries, SEC filings, official company statements

**Examples:**
- Companies House (UK)
- SEC EDGAR (US)
- Official press releases
- Company investor relations pages
- Annual reports

**Confidence Multiplier:** 1.0

### Tier 2: Professional Data Platforms (High Credibility)

**What:** Established business intelligence platforms, major tech publications

**Examples:**
- Crunchbase
- PitchBook
- LinkedIn (official company pages)
- TechCrunch, Bloomberg, Reuters
- Dealroom, Tracxn

**Confidence Multiplier:** 0.9

### Tier 3: Secondary Sources (Moderate Credibility)

**What:** News aggregators, industry publications, professional profiles

**Examples:**
- Tech.eu, Sifted, EU-Startups
- Individual LinkedIn profiles
- Company blogs
- Industry analyst reports
- Glassdoor, Indeed

**Confidence Multiplier:** 0.7

### Tier 4: Unverified Sources (Low Credibility - Verify Before Use)

**What:** Social media, forums, anonymous sources

**Examples:**
- Twitter/X posts
- Reddit discussions
- Anonymous reviews
- Wikipedia (use to find primary sources only)

**Confidence Multiplier:** 0.4

## Technical Co-Founder Detection

> **Critical for BD targeting - determines technical credibility and build capability.**

### Definition

A **Technical Co-Founder** is a founder who:
1. Has a technical degree (CS, Engineering, Physics, Mathematics) OR
2. Has significant technical work experience (software engineering, CTO, tech lead) OR
3. Built the initial product/prototype themselves

### Detection Methodology

```
1. Identify all founders from:
   - Company website "About/Team" page
   - LinkedIn company page
   - Crunchbase/PitchBook founder list
   - Press releases announcing founding

2. For each founder, verify:
   □ Education background (LinkedIn Education section)
   □ Work history (LinkedIn Experience section)
   □ Published technical content (GitHub, blogs, papers)
   □ Speaking at technical conferences
   □ Technical certifications or awards

3. Classification:
   - TECHNICAL: Degree in STEM + technical role history
   - TECHNICAL-ADJACENT: Business degree but CTO/technical role
   - NON-TECHNICAL: Business/other background, non-technical roles
   - UNKNOWN: Insufficient information to determine
```

### Output Format

```markdown
## Founding Team

| Name | Role | Background | Technical? | Confidence |
|------|------|------------|------------|------------|
| Jane Smith | CEO/Co-founder | CS @ Cambridge, SWE @ Google (5yr) | Yes | 5 |
| John Doe | COO/Co-founder | MBA @ LBS, McKinsey (3yr) | No | 5 |

**Technical Co-Founder Status:** Yes
**Confidence:** 5/5
**Evidence:** Jane Smith holds CS degree from Cambridge and worked as Software Engineer at Google for 5 years before founding.
```

## B2B vs B2C Classification

> **Critical for BD targeting - determines sales approach and relevance.**

### Classification Criteria

| Signal | B2B Indicator | B2C Indicator |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Pricing** | "Contact sales", enterprise tiers, per-seat | Public pricing, freemium, subscription |
| **Language** | "Teams", "Enterprise", "Business" | "Personal", "Individual", "Family" |
| **Features** | SSO, admin console, API, integrations | Social features, personal dashboard |
| **Customers** | Company logos, case studies | User testimonials, app store reviews |
| **Sales** | Demo requests, sales team | Self-serve signup, app download |
| **Content** | Whitepapers, ROI calculators | How-to guides, lifestyle content |

### Classification Output

```markdown
## Business Model

**Classification:** B2B / B2C / B2B2C / Hybrid
**Confidence:** [1-5]/5

**Evidence:**
- [Signal 1 with source]
- [Signal 2 with source]
- [Signal 3 with source]

**Target Customer:** [Description of ideal customer]
**Revenue Model:** [SaaS/Transactional/Marketplace/etc.]
```

```
                                      ↓

                                      ↓

│  PHASE 5: CUSTOMER RESEARCH                                                  │

                                      ↓

│  → Include all confidence scores                                             │

│  → Add BD recommendations                                                    │
│  → Save to product/research/companies/{company-slug}.md                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Example Confidence Scoring

### High Confidence Example (Score: 5)

**Fact:** Company founded in 2019

**Verification:**
- Companies House: Incorporated 15 March 2019 (Tier 1)
- Crunchbase: Founded 2019 (Tier 2)
- LinkedIn: Founded 2019 (Tier 2)
- Company website: "Founded in 2019" (Tier 2)

**Consensus:** 4 sources agree → **Confidence: 5**

### Moderate Confidence Example (Score: 3)

**Fact:** Company has 50-100 employees

**Verification:**
- LinkedIn: 51-200 employees (Tier 3)
- Crunchbase: 50 employees (Tier 2)

**Consensus:** 2 sources with slight variance → **Confidence: 3**

### Low Confidence Example (Score: 2)

**Fact:** Company revenue is $5M ARR

**Verification:**
- Tech news article: "reportedly $5M ARR" citing "sources" (Tier 3)
- No other sources found

**Consensus:** Single unverified source → **Confidence: 2**

## Handling Common Challenges

### Stealth Mode Companies
1. Note limited public information
2. Focus on founder backgrounds
3. Check for patents, job postings
4. Flag as "Limited Public Information"

### Conflicting Information
1. Document all versions found
2. Weight by source tier
3. Note the conflict in report
4. Use most reliable source value

### Recently Founded Companies
1. May have limited funding/customer data
2. Focus on founder credentials
3. Look for accelerator participation
4. Note early stage appropriately

### Non-UK Companies
1. Use equivalent registries (SEC for US, etc.)
2. Adjust source expectations
3. Note jurisdiction in report
